Prambanan Temple

Prambanan Temple is the largest Hindu temple complex in Indonesia and one of the most magnificent ancient monuments in Southeast Asia. Built in the 9th century, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its towering spires and intricate stone carvings that depict stories from the Ramayana epic. Surrounded by lush gardens, Prambanan offers a breathtaking view, especially at sunrise and sunset. Visitors can explore the main temples dedicated to Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma, as well as hundreds of smaller shrines scattered throughout the complex.
